Project Abstract

Bridging citizens and authorities
with on-device AI.

Pakistan's urban communities face a structural gap between citizens and local authorities. Civic issues like potholes and garbage piles persist for weeks because citizens lack a low-friction channel to report them, complaints get mis-routed, and there's no accountability when nothing happens.

CivicSense addresses this gap through a Flutter-based mobile application that combines on-device image classification using a fine-tuned MobileNetV2 model, GPS-driven polygon routing to Union Council administrators, and a verification workflow that requires citizens to confirm or dispute the resolution of their complaints.

The system supports three roles — citizen, UC administrator, and super administrator — each with role-based access control, privacy-preserving data masking, and a complete audit trail. Our model achieves 92.3% validation accuracy across three classes (pothole, garbage, normal) with sub-200ms inference time on mid-range Android devices.
